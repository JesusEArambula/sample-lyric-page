(Don't Fear) The Reaper

by Blue Oyster Cult

Blue Oyster Cult's (Don't Fear) The Reaper is a 1976 rock song about accepting the inevitability of death and the belief that love transcends physical existance. The song was written by Buck Dharma during a health scare, it argues against fearing mortaility, using imagery of Romeo and Juliet to suggest a peaceful reunion in the afterlife.

Key Information

  • Release Date: 1976
  • Writer: Donald "Buck Dharma" Roeser
  • Producer: David Lucas, Murray. Krugman, Sandy Pearlman
  • Genre(s): Hard Rock, Soft Rock, Psychedelic Rock, Classic Rock
